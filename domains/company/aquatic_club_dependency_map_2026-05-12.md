## Company Objective

The talk should become the Q2 forcing function: every remaining Q2 work item should either directly improve the talk or support the assets, proof, web/social presence, follow-up paths, and readiness needed to make the talk land well.

The talk should help people:
- Buy into the miniBIOTA story.
- Feel emotionally connected to the project.
- Understand the big picture of what the build means and why it matters.
- Make their own novel connections to the project.
- Leave with curiosity, awe, and a sense of future possibility.
- Know how to stay connected through following, networking, or direct support.

## Critical Dependencies

### Script Before Deck Polish

The rough script and act map should come before heavy slide design. Otherwise the deck can become busy before the story is stable.

Near-term dependency:
- Brand/Content refine the working title, one-liner, opening hook, and act map.
- Content assembles rough rehearsal script v0.
- Rehearsal begins before full visual polish.

### Claim Review Before Final Deck Lock

Research and Hardware review need to happen before the final deck locks. This protects against overclaiming closed-system, species, population, telemetry, sensor, or future capability language.

Near-term dependency:
- Content provides draft claims and proof targets.
- Research labels ecological certainty.
- Hardware labels engineering current-vs-future boundaries.

### CTA Before QR / Web Finalization

The QR destination should not be finalized until Brand and Growth clarify the support invitation. Web should draft the minimum surface early, but public copy and sponsor-interest language need approval before going live.

Near-term dependency:
- Brand drafts CTA language.
- Growth defines follow-up categories.
- Financials provides guardrails for any support/sponsor framing.
- Web drafts the QR destination and validates the flow.

### Social/Profile Refresh Before Talk Window

Social bios, banners, profile images, and descriptions should align with the talk message before people look Josue up after the talk. The update itself remains approval-gated.

Near-term dependency:

Content needs proof media early enough that missing visuals do not become a deck blocker.

Near-term dependency:

## Readiness Risks

Highest risks:
- Not enough rehearsal.
- Slide deck not ready.
- Rough script delayed.
- Proof media scattered or hard to find.
- Public/support language becomes too vague, too commercial, or too commitment-heavy.
- Ecological or engineering claims overstate current state.
- QR/web/support path exists too late to test.
- Social/profile update happens after the audience has already looked for the project.

Mitigations:
- Pull script and rehearsal earlier than deck polish.
- Keep a rough deck skeleton with placeholders before final proof media.
- Require Research/Hardware review before final deck lock.
- Keep CTA as a simple invitation until Growth/Financials/Brand approve stronger language.
- Use a minimum QR destination draft before rehearsal close.
- Treat social/profile work as Brand-owned and approval-gated.
