## Strategy Conclusions

1. App Planner/Supabase is the live project/task/program runtime. Markdown can store strategy, context, decisions, and operating rules, but must not compete as current task-status truth.
2. Company owns Planner strategy and governance for Company work: Programs/Operations meaning, project-management cadence, dependency interpretation, review rhythm, and cross-domain closeout expectations.
3. App owns Planner implementation: schema, migrations, UI, renderer behavior, source files, write behavior, and Supabase-backed workflow execution.
4. Domain projects remain domain-owned execution units. Program membership coordinates work but does not transfer implementation ownership or approval authority away from the domain.
5. Planner status is operational workflow state. It does not create or prove roadmap commitments, launch targets, public promises, financial commitments, sponsor obligations, legal decisions, or publication state.
6. Current Planner status reads are needed before choosing live next work, checking blockers, or marking tasks complete. This session did not perform live reads; it was a strategy/ownership review only.
7. Planner project/task/program creates, edits, links, scheduling changes, recurrence changes, status changes, done/reopen actions, and archives are live Supabase writes and require explicit approval.
